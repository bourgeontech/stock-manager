Kulacharam

The three qualities of Satva, Rajas and Tamas are contained in Brahmashakti which is ever-immersed in the Nirguna Brahman. Kulacharam is a way of worshiping that threefold spiritual power according to their own qualities. There are ten types of worshiping methods depending on the quality of the qualities present in the worshipper. Here at Parakkunnath, the Bhagwati is an embodiment of the Rajjo Tamasika quality.

The Nagas are propitiated for their blessings either to beget progeny or to secure health and wealth; to ward off evil effects; and, also for protection against drought and such other disasters. The Nagas are associated with gods, ancestors (Pitris) and other super-beings!

Dharshan Timings

Darshan timings: Parakkunnath Temple is open from 5 AM till 11 AM. Darshan is allowed all through the day except brief intervals of time when certain rituals or decorations are being performed. Evening the opening time is limited to 5 PM to 6:30 PM

  • 5: 00 AM --- Opening
  • 7: 00 AM --- Poojas for Bhagavathy and Chathan
  • 8: 00 AM --- Conducting of offerings
  • 9: 00 AM --- Pooja for Bhagavathy

    • Prior to this pooja, the offering reciept of Shatru Samhara Pushpanjali, Shatru Samhara Muttu, Mangalya Muttu should be given to shantis

  • 11: 00 AM --- Guruthi and closing of temple
  • 5:00 PM --- Opening of temple
  • 6:30 PM --- Closing of temple
